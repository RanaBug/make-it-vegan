MAKE IT VEGAN

Fish Swaps

While it may at first seem like a tricky one to swap, there are plenty of fish alternatives available, including seaweed, tofu, and banana blossom - and as well as making convincing substitutions, they often have additional health benefits.

Tofu Fish

Tofu fish, or ‘tofish’ makes a good substitute, and not only because of its white colour. Battered ‘tofish’ with chips is an alternative to a traditional fish supper - just wrap a nori seaweed sheet around a firm piece of tofu, then coat in batter before frying.
Tofu also has the benefit of being high in protein and calcium.

Smoked Carrot Salmon

With carrots and the addition of a few seasonings - including soy sauce, apple cider vinegar and paprika - you can make a surprisingly tasty vegan smoked salmon substitute. This can be enjoyed with vegan cream cheese on a bagel, or as sushi.

Jackfruit Tuna

This tinned fruit makes a great swap for a tuna mayonnaise sandwich filling or tuna salad, as jackfruit has a similar flaky texture. You just need to separate it with a fork before marinating or cooking, flavour with nori or soy sauce to give it a ‘fishy taste’, then add vegan mayonnaise as required.

Banana Blossom Fish

Banana blossom is the fleshy flower of the banana plant and has recently become a popular fish alternative. Like jackfruit, it’s available in cans and has a neutral flavour that makes it ideal for adding seaweed or other fish substitute flavorings. Its chunky, flaky texture is a good dupe for cod or a fish filet.
